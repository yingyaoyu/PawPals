Main Features

Our Main Features

Choose an Endangered Animal

After setting up an account, users are prompted to choose to raise an endangered animal of their choice. For instance, on this page, Arctic Wolf has been chosen.

Home Page

Users would then be directed to a Home Page with their chosen animal and name presented in the center, with the friendship level indicated by the percentage next to the heart icon on the top right corner, and with directions to other pages (Communities, Home, Pals, News) shown at the bottom

News page

The news page allows users to access the latest news and in-depth articles pertaining to endangered species, raising public awareness and encouraging for active participation in preservation initiatives.

Building Friendship Level through Tasks & Quizzes!

On this Page, users can deepen their bond and friendship levels with their chosen endangered animal by engaging in relevant and fun tasks and quizzes. These interactions are designed not only to provide enjoyment but also to enhance the user's knowledge of the animal's characteristics and habitat.

Tasks Page

On the task page, users have the opportunity to engage in a variety of impactful activities, including walking 10,000 steps, dedicating an hour to volunteer work, or purchasing a reusable water bottle. Each of these tasks, whether through direct or indirect actions, supports the conservation of biodiversity.

Quiz page

On the quiz page, users are encouraged to answer targeted quizzes related to their selected endangered species to heighten awareness about this critical issue. With each quiz completed successfully, the friendship level will also increase, serving as an incentive.

Pals Page

On the pals page, users have the option to choose and name to another endangered animal they wish to adopt. Users can raise a new animal once their friendship/affectoin level with the current adopted animal has reached 100%.

Community page

On the community page, users can view the status of their friends' adopted animals, fostering connections and interactions among each other. This collaborative and engaging space further motivates them to collectively contribute more to the care of the animals.
